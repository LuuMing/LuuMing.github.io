Child Safety Standards

Zero-tolerance policy. 阿瓦隆面杀宝 (Avalon Helper), published by MingLu, strictly prohibits child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) content of any kind on or through its services. This standard is published in compliance with Google Play's Child Safety Standards policy.

1. Scope

These Child Safety Standards apply to 阿瓦隆面杀宝 (Avalon Helper) — a single-player assistant tool for the social-deduction board game Avalon. The application is developed and maintained by MingLu (the "Developer"). While the application does not host user-to-user messaging or public content feeds, the Developer adopts and publishes these Standards to demonstrate its commitment to child safety across every surface of the product.

2. Prohibited Content and Conduct

The following are strictly prohibited within 阿瓦隆面杀宝 (Avalon Helper) and any associated Developer-operated channels:

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM), in any form, including but not limited to imagery, audio, video, text, AI-generated content, or computer-generated content that sexualizes minors.
  • Content that sexualizes, exploits, endangers, or otherwise harms children.
  • Grooming, sextortion, trafficking, or solicitation of minors.
  • Encouragement, normalization, or promotion of child sexual abuse or exploitation.
  • Sharing personal information of minors without lawful authorization.

3. Age Policy

阿瓦隆面杀宝 (Avalon Helper) is not directed to children under the age of 13 and the Developer does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If the Developer becomes aware that personal information from a child under 13 has been collected, that information will be deleted promptly.

Anyone who encounters suspected child sexual abuse or exploitation material, or any violation of these Standards, in connection with 阿瓦隆面杀宝 (Avalon Helper) may report it to the Developer using any of the following channels:

  • Email: 784315443@qq.com — reports are monitored and acknowledged within 2 business days.
  • Subject line: please use [CSAE Report] so the report is prioritized.
  • In-app: users may contact the Developer via the feedback option in the application's settings screen.

Reports may be submitted anonymously. The Developer will review every report received and take appropriate action, including removal of offending content where applicable, account restriction, and escalation to law enforcement.

5. Response and Enforcement

When a report is substantiated, the Developer will:

  • Remove or disable the offending content immediately.
  • Preserve evidence as required by applicable law.
  • Report confirmed CSAM to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) CyberTipline where required, and to other relevant authorities in accordance with applicable law (including, where applicable, 18 U.S.C. § 2258A in the United States).
  • Cooperate with lawful requests from law enforcement and child-safety organizations.
